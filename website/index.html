Purpose of IT in modern business(1.1):

"Starting in the early 1980s with the first desktop computers, information technology has played an important part in the U.S. and global economies. Companies rely on IT for fast communications, data processing and market intelligence. IT plays an integral role in every industry, helping companies improve business processes, achieve cost efficiencies, drive revenue growth and maintain a competitive advantage in the marketplace." [1]

From product development to globalisation IT has played a huge part in how businesses operate in today's modern world.

IT can help speed up the time it takes for new products to reach the market. A company could produce a product requirement or specification by looking up information from databases, sales representatives and even the customers the product is intended for. CAD (Computer assisted design) helps to speed up the decision making process and also the manufacturing process itself. Fom advancements in microprocessors to efficient drug delivery systems, IT can help a business respond rapidly to ever changing customer needs. [2]

Another way IT helps businesses is with security. A Lot of businesses are constantly under threat, whether it is someone trying to steal company ideas or attacks that can bring a website offline, resulting in the potential loss of money, Technology can be used to protect the business. From financial data to confidential data, IT can help to keep ideas and data from the competition. With passwords and encryption a business can make sure none of its projects can be stolen by the competition or have financial information stolen. [3]

Looking at all this you can see why IT is important for businesses, not only does it help the business to compete in an ever growing, 24/7 global marketplace, it also helps companies come up with new ideas quicker and then also to make those ideas a reality faster than would normally be possible. Customers can contact the company quicker resulting in happy customers who expect all problems to be resolved as quickly and efficiently as possible and also keep those ideas safe from competition. It helps make sure their finances are all in the correct order and safe from attacks while constantly updating employee records making sure employees are working efficiently and also being paid the correct wage automatically each month. This all helps to make everything a business does simpler and more efficient.

GDPR(1.7):

The general Data Protection Regulations came into effect on the 25th of may 2018 and apply to all organisations, including both charities and voluntary organisations that process anyone's personal data. If an organisation holds personal data, which is anything from contact information, ethnicity, religious belief to bank or credit card information it applies to them. There are the right to be informed, right of access, right to rectification, right to be forgotten, right to restrict processing, right to data portability, right to object and rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling.

It allows more transparency with how companies collect and use data and informs the people if there is a breach that would result in a risk to the rights and freedom to an individual. People have the right to assess how the data is being used, the right to be forgotten, which means the company has to halt processing the data.

If a company is found to be in breach of the GDPR they can be fined up to 4% of their annual global turnover or 20 million euros, whichever is greater, which are the maximum fines for the most severe infringements. Both controllers and processors are included so cloud based companies are not exempt. [11]
